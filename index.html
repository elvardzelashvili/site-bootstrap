State parks/City parks

There are seven state parks within the confines of New York City, including Clay Pit Ponds State Park Preserve, a natural area that includes extensive riding trails, and Riverbank State Park, a 28-acre (110,000 m2) facility that rises 69 feet (21 m) over the Hudson River New York City has over 28,000 acres (110 km2) of municipal parkland and 14 miles (23 km) of public beaches.[234] The largest municipal park in the city is Pelham Bay Park in the Bronx, with 2,772 acres (1,122 ha)
